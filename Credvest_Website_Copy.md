## SECTION 4: WHY PROJECTS LOSE VELOCITY

**Section Title:** Why projects lose velocity.

**Intro:** Most projects don't fail because of the product. They fail because the system selling it is broken.

---

**01 — Pre-Launch Misalignment**
Multiple agencies. Diluted positioning. Delayed market entry. By the time the project launches, the narrative is already fractured — and the first 90 days are wasted fixing what should have been aligned from day one.

**02 — Channel Dilution**
Over-reliance on brokers reduces margins and distorts brand narrative. When ten channel partners tell ten different stories about your project, the buyer trusts none of them.

**03 — Experience Gaps**
Marketing promises don't match on-ground sales experience. The brochure says one thing. The site visit feels like another. That gap erodes buyer trust faster than any competitor can.

## SECTION 10: FAQ

**Section Title:** Frequently Asked Questions

---

**Q: How does Credvest work with developers?**
A: We operate as an extended arm of the developer, managing every stage of the sales lifecycle — from project positioning and launch planning to on-ground execution, channel management, and customer closure. One partner replaces multiple disconnected vendors.

**Q: Is Credvest a brokerage?**
A: No. We are a structured operations partner. Sales at Credvest is treated as a system — with dedicated teams, real-time reporting, and long-term mandate partnerships. We don't broker deals. We build and run the engine that sells projects.

**Q: Do your teams work under our brand?**
A: Yes. White-label execution is core to how we operate. Our teams represent the developer's brand at every buyer touchpoint — site visits, calls, collateral, and closures.

**Q: What cities do you operate in?**
A: Bangalore and Hyderabad are our primary markets, with deep micro-market expertise across both cities. We are expanding to additional markets on a mandate basis.

**Q: How is performance tracked?**
A: Real-time. We provide live pipeline visibility, weekly performance reviews, and structured reporting — not monthly PDFs. Developers see what's moving, what's stuck, and what needs to change as it happens.

**Q: What types of projects do you manage?**
A: Residential and commercial developments — from single-tower projects to large township mandates. We structure our teams and systems based on the scale and complexity of each project.
